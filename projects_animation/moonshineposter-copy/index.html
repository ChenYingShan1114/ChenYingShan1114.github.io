Control light by music real-time

2025-03-14 intern project group work Unreal Engine ToughDesigner Virtual Production

During the time I become an technical art intern in Moonshine Studio which is one of the biggest animation company in Taiwan, I cooperate with many people whose backgrounds are totally very different from me. In the company, the artists made original 2D or 3D animations, motion design, post-production for movies or dramas, such like dynamic simulation, FBX…, interactive media, and also research and design new technique, such like AI, which is useful in the pipeline of any products. However, I act as intern in a special team which combine realistic and virtualistic to implement virtual production in MoonShine XR studio. They are the first company that introduced virtual production in Taiwan.

The virtual studio is constructed by multiple led panels (walls and ceiling) and these panels are able to control brightness, lighting position and animation. Instead of using traditional chroma-key techniques in shooting, we now have a better and even faster solution.

Chroma-key technique vs. Virtual production

Aspect Chroma-key technique Virtual Production
Scene and Light Flexibility Low Hight
Scene and Light Cost High Low
Actor's fit into the Scene Hard Easy
Post-production Cost High Low

Therefore, we use Unreal Engine to generate a virtual scene and project to the led panels in the studio. Then, the real camera position in the studio is sent to Unreal Engine to implement real-time rendering and project it on the scene. The actor can stand in front the camera with some lighting, then finally capture the picture or video. After that, post-processing makes the result better. This technique not only cost down moving fee of crews and actors, but also makes their working hour more stable.

The following series of posters is the final work of my team. Our topic is “Medieval relics”. My main work is building the scene in Unreal Engine and control the computer and the image the led panels show in the studio.
